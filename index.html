John Coltrane

Musician of a Generation

John Coltrane was one of the most influencial tenor saxophonists and jazz musicians of the 20th century.

Timeline

  • 1926 - Birth
  • 1939 - Coltrane's father dies, a tramautic event that would affect his music later on. In that same year, he would pick up his first saxophone.
  • 1949 - First recording session with legendary trumpeter Dizzy Gillespie, one of the earliest musical milestones for the saxophonist.
  • 1955 - First recording session with Miles Davis' band. This is the most important band he played in during the first half of his career.
  • 1956 - Recording session for Sonny Rollins' album Tenor Madness. Rollins is another prolific tenor saxophonist.
  • 1957 - First recording session with Thelonius Monk, an early lankmark jazz pianist.
  • 1957 - Blue Train: One of Coltrane's first albums. It was hugely successful.
  • 1959 - Kind of Blue: This Miles Davis album was landmark transition piece for jazz. Coltranes' playing is innovative, pointing to a new way to play saxophone. Among other reasons, this album is important for its major shift in harmonic approach.
  • 1959 - Giant Steps - the title track off of this album marks another dramatic change in harmoinc approach.
  • 1960 - My Favorite Things - Coltrane covers the Sound of Music hit. To this day is it his most famous track.
  • 1961 - Africa Brass is recorded.
  • 1962 - Coltrane forms his "Classic Quartet" with Jimmy Garrison(bass), McCoy Tyner(piano), and Elvin Jones(drums). This group, considered by some to be the most important, recorded all of the notable ablums during Coltrane's late period.
  • 1964 - A Love Supreme is recorded. Many consider this his greatest work. It serves as a tribute to God, which was the primary motivator in Coltrane's second half of his career.
  • 1967 - Coltrane had his last recording session in March.
  • 1967 - Olatunji Concert - Coltrane's last live recorded performance.
  • 1967 - Coltrane dies of liver disease at the age of 41.
  • 2014 - Coltrane is admitted into the Grammy Hall of Fame.
